The Strategy

Jesse Livermore never analyzed balance sheets. He read price. His breakout rules — enter on 20-day highs confirmed by 50MA and 200MA, exit on 20-day low breaks — have been running financial markets for over a century. We test whether they still work, and where.

Research Hypothesis
Livermore's alpha is driven by human emotional cycles in price — not by what a company does. If true, the strategy should produce higher Sharpe ratios on high-volatility stocks, regardless of sector, because fear and greed amplify breakout signals.

Strategy vs. Buy & Hold

Buy & Hold means buying on day one and holding until the end date without any trades. This is the baseline — the minimum return you could get with zero effort. Livermore's strategy must beat this to justify the complexity.
